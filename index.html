Research Interests

My research focuses on **high-energy transients**, particularly **Gamma-Ray Bursts (GRBs)**, compact binary mergers, and collapsars. I utilize **multi-wavelength observations** and **machine learning** to explore their origins and significance in astrophysics.

Classification of GRBs

Using **machine learning**, I analyze GRB properties beyond the traditional short/long classification. Techniques like **UMAP embeddings and clustering** help uncover **hidden structures in GRB populations**.

Merger Rate Estimations

In collaboration with **Shahvath**, I estimate **compact binary merger rates** using GRB and **gravitational wave data**. This work refines predictions for future **GW detections** and constrains merger evolution.

Short GRB Progenitors

Conducting **optical/NIR follow-ups**, I study the environments of **short GRBs**, investigating their progenitors and potential **kilonova counterparts** from compact binary mergers.
